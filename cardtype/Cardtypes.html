The Card Types

Each card has a card type.

It will tell you how they behave
when they are played.

Every Card Has ONE Card Type

(At least !!)

Card types are what determine how a card acts when it is cast. Will it stay on the battlefield? Will it have an effect and go to the graveyard shortly after taking effect? Can you cast it in response to another spell? Will it have a power and toughness stat? Come find out!

Lands

These represent the locations the player have under their control. We can usually put one of them onto the battlefield per turn. We can tap (turn sideways) these types of cards to produce mana of their color. For example, this Island can be turned sideways to produce one blue mana.
Lands are played during the main phase. These do not have power and toughness stats nor mana costs.
Lands are your resources.

Creatures

Lorewise, these cards represent the minions and creatures the players command to fight for them. Creatures, unlike lands, have the capacity to attack the enemy and block enemy creatures' attacks.
When a creature attacks and is not blocked, the creature deals damage equal to its power to the opponent's health. When a creature is blocked, it deals damage equal to its power to the defending creature's health and vice versa. When a creature's health is reduced to zero or less than zero, they are put in the graveyard.
Summoning sickness refers to the fact that creatures, unless otherwise stated, cannot attack or tap directly after being played.
Like lands, creatures are played during the main phase.

Sorcery

Sorceries are the spells you cast. These are not permanents and they are not placed on the battlefield after they are cast. When their mana cost is paid, they do what they are intended to do and are put in the graveyard.
Similar to lands and creatures, they may only be cast during the main phase.

Instant

Instants, like sorceries, do what they are supposed to do when they are cast and go to the graveyard right after — the key difference being the fact that you can cast instants in response to another spell. Note that instants follow the first in, last out rule.

Enchantments

Enchantments resemble spells that have a lasting effect. These are a type of permanent that have a mana cost, like a creature, but does not have a power and toughness stat, like a land. As all permanents do, enchantments stay on board and, usually, have a lasting effect on the battlefield.
An Aura is a common enchantment subtype. These types of enchantments attach onto other permanents and have an effect on what they attach to.
Like lands and creatures, enchantments are played during the main phase.

Artifact

Artifacts represent enchanted items, pieces of gear, and magical devices. Like enchantments, these permanents have a mana cost but do not, usually, have a power and toughness stat. This card type tends to be broad with what they can do, so they can do a lot of things. They are kinda the Swiss knives of card types!
Like most permanents, artifacts can only be played during the main phase.
