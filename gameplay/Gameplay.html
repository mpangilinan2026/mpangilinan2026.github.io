How to Play MTG

Let's get into the specifics.

Game Actions

These are the types actions you take in a game.

Tap and Untap

Tapping is the action of turning a card sideways. You do this to a land to make it produce mana, and to a creature to make it attack. Tapping may also be included in the cost of an ability. Once a permanent is tapped, it can't be tapped again until it is untapped. All permanents are untapped at the beginning of a player's turn.

Attacking and Blocking

In order to win a game, you must reduce your opponent's life to 0. The most common way to do this is to remove your opponent's creatures with instants and sorceries, and attack them with your own creatures.
A player can attack on their turn's combat phase. During this phase, one is tasked to choose their creatures that will attack, and which opponent each of their creatures will attack (if you have multiple opponents). You tap these creatures unless said otherwise. Already tapped creatures cannot attack.
Your opponent will then choose which of their creatures will block and what creature will each of their creatures block. After this, :
  1. Attacking creatures that aren't blocked will damage their opponent
  2. Attacking creatures that are blocked deal and receive damage from their blockers
The controller of the attacking creature decides how damage is distributed when an attacking creature is blocked by multiple creatures. Creatures reduced to zero or less health are put in the graveyard. Damaged, but not dead, creatures regenerate to full health at the end of the turn.

Parts of a Turn

Flow of Actions

This is the flow of events in a turn. The player to take the first turn may be determined by dice, coin toss, or rock-paper-scissors. It depends on the players really.

Begin Phase

  1. Untap Step
Here, you untap all of your permanents that were tapped. If none were tapped, skip this step.
  1. Upkeep
Although rarely, players may cast instants and activate abilities on this step. This step is also mentioned in a few cards. These cards usually trigger an action such as card draw â€” "At the beginning of your upkeep,.."
  1. Draw Step
Next, you are to draw a card from your library. In a two-player game, the first player to take a turn skips this step. This is to make up for the advantage that they are to go first.

First Main Phase

A player's main phase usually starts off with a land, as it is our primary resource in Magic. Remember that you may play only one land per turn. Then, the player may choose to play any number of creatures, sorceries, enchantments, instants, and artifacts â€” as long as they may pay their mana costs. Opponents may play instants and activate abilities in response to these.

Combat Phase

  1. Start of Combat
Players may cast instants and activate abilities.
  1. Declare Attackers
The player chooses which creatures will attack, if any, and which opponent they will attack. They tap these creatures. Then, players may cast instants and activate abilities.
  1. Declare Blockers
The defending player chooses which of their creatures will block and which creature their creatures will block. Remember that multiple creatures may block a single creature, but the attacking player chooses how the damage is spread among blockers. Then, players may cast instants and activate abilities.
  1. Combat Damage
  1. Attacking creatures that aren't blocked will damage their opponent
  2. Attacking creatures that are blocked deal and receive damage from their blockers
Once the amount of damage dealt is finalized, combat damage is all dealt at the same time. Then, players may cas instants and activate abilities.
  1. End of Combat
Players may cast instants and activate abilities.

Second Main Phase

Similar to your first main phase, you may play any type of spell during this phase. Your opponents may still play instants and activate abilities. You may also play a land if you didn't in your first main phase.

Ending Phase

  1. End Step
Abilities that trigger on the end step trigger now. These are indicated by "at the beginning of your end step." Then, players may cast instants and sorceries.
  1. Clean Up
Players with more than 7 cards in their hand discard back to hand size. All damaged creatures are healed back to full. All "until end of turn" events end. No one may cast instants and activate sorceries.
