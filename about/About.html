My First Standard Deck!

Simic Flash

The first Magic deck I owned was a Simic Flash deck. Simic meant that the deck had two colors: Blue and Green. Flash refers to the keyword. Cards with these keywords allow the card to be cast as if it were an instant. This meant that you would usually play your cards on your opponent's turns.
This synergized very well with Nightpack Ambusher's third ability. Over all, you used your blue spells to stifle your opponent's plans and protect your green flash spells.

My First Commander Deck!

Lady of -1/-1 Counters and Snake Tokens

Commander is a Magic Format where in you have 1 commander, a 99-card deck, and 3 opponents. My First commander was Hapatra, the Vizier of Poisons.
This deck focused very heavily on -1/-1 counters that lowered opponent creature's power and toughness simultaneously. Additionally, the deck had a token sub-theme.
The deck won by slowly chipping away at the enemy's units while growing your own army of snake tokens.

My First Pauper Deck!

GW Auras

Pauper is a Magic Format where in you have 60 cards and all of those cards are of common rarity.
This deck focuses on putting aura enchantments that buff your creatures on cheap hexproof and untargettable creatures, such as the Slippery Bogle. Hexproof refers to the keyword that prevents a creature from being targetted by enemies.
The deck won by overwhelming your opponent with buffed, small, and hard to kill creatures.
